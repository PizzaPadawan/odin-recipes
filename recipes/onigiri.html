Okaka Onigiri (Japanese Bonito Flake Rice Balls)

Description

Onigiri is a delicious Japanese dish made from rice, formed into a triangular ball. It is either filled in the middle one of many assorted fillings, or additional ingredients are mixed in with the rice.

A small strip of nori (seaweed paper} is usually wrapped around the bottom to make it easier to hold while eating. Think of it as the Japanese version of a sandwich!

In this recipe, we'll be making Okaka Onigiri, where the rice is mixed with a umami combination of bonito flake, tsuyu sauce, and sesame seeds.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups uncooked short-grain white rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup rice wine vinegar
  • 4 cups ice water (set aside in a bowl)
  • 2 tsp salt (set aside in a bowl)
  • 2 tbsp katsuobushi (bonito flake)
  • 1 tbsp tsuyu sauce
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 2 sheets nori, cut into 1/2 inch strips

Steps

Preparing rice mixture:

  1. Wash your rice twice using a fine mesh strainer and a bowl.
  2. Add your 2 cups washed rice and 2 cups water to your rice cooker and set to Cook.
  3. Once your rice cooker has flipped to the Keep Warm setting, add 1/4 cup rice wine vinegar, 1/4 cup sugar, and 1 tbsp salt to a small saucepan and heat on medium-high, stirring until combined.
  4. Remove your sushi rice seasoning from the heat and set aside
  5. In a bowl, stir to combine your bonito flake, tsuyu sauce, and sesame seeds.
  6. After your rice has rested on the Keep Warm setting for 10 minutes, transfer the rice to a large wooden bowl (or a hangiri, if you have one) and drizzle your sushi rice seasoning over the rice. Stir to combine.
  7. Add bonito flake mixture to the rice and stir to combine. Cover sushi rice bowl with a damp cloth and wait 15 - 20 minutes, or until rice has cooled to room temperature.

Forming onigiri balls:

  1. Once rice has cooled, line up the water bowl, salt, rice mixture, and nori sheeets.
  2. To form onigiri, first wet your hands in the ice water, then take a pinch of salt and rub it on your hands.
  3. Pick up a large handful of rice and form it into a triangular ball.
  4. Once the ball is formed, wrap a nori sheet around the bottom of the onigiri.
  5. Repeat until all rice is used, and enjoy!
